Information Technology Project


Overview

An external mobile or computer application interface used to organize and inform users of a shared household, the internal status of dishes (clean or dirty) and the current capacity of space in the Dishwasher through a wide array of easy-to-understand user-selected prompts and options that are viewed by local members or those who use the same dishwasher. It attempts to mitigate the mixing of different states of dishes (clean or dirty) and over stacking in the dishwasher.


Motivation

Research shows that ‘11.92 million Australians (58%) now own a dishwasher, up over 2.2 million(+8pps) from four years ago (Ray Morgan 2019, para 1). Now that report was from 2019, so It is not too unreasonable to assume that roughly the same percentage of Australians if not slightly more own and use a dishwasher, especially after a widespread pandemic resulting in people staying at home more now than ever before.

The app will take advantage of the widespread android smartphone communications by giving users robust options for effective and constant communication allowing them to plan and organize around the use of the dishwasher. Setting out to eliminate things like wasteful dishwasher cycles and scenarios where other members of a household require dishes which are still been cleaned and many others. Some future improvements like being able to put it on at off-peak electricity usage times to save money is something which should be explored at a later point intime.

Detailed Description

Upon application installation or initial launch, you will be greeted with a list of options and filters that will allow you to specifically pick what and how you want to use the application. The available filters and options include the number of people that will be using the dishwasher/application, how many times and how frequently on average a day/hour/week/month you use the dishwasher, notifications settings for other users in your local group. The application will only plan and be able to support one dishwasher per household (at this stage). These options will be implemented to appear and interact somewhat like group fitness apps and others of the same liking. The effect these filters will have on the overall experience will be is the pre-determined or autocomplete (but still customizable) suggestions and options. An example of why such as system would exist is to prevent every time a user goes to change status, they do not need to select from such an overwhelming number of options that might not apply to their living situation. Of course, in saying that users will still have the option to show advanced options or “more options” when updating and organizing planned dishwasher cycles with their group.

After setting up the filters and options, users will be asked to create a private group with a unique QR invite code allowing others of their choosing to receive notifications, group messages and view a timeline. The timeline will contain information such as when the dishwasher was last emptied and at what time, what type of dishes were unloaded and or loaded with a rough percentage of “Plates, Bowls, Glasses etc”.

When putting on a dirty dishwasher load users will be able to go into the application and set a timer for the dishwasher, setting the time the same as on the dishwasher load will send a notification to the other users in the said group making sure no matter if no one’s at the house that whoever gets home first will be able to open the app and check the timer in the app to see when the load finished or if it's still going based on the time or notification sent. After a user also unloads the dishwasher, they will be able to select an option to confirm the load has been unpacked, this will also either send a notification or be a checkable status in-app. If not, all dishes are unpacked or packed the user will be able to select an option specifying a partial completion of the process and what state the dishwasher was in upon selecting that option (Clean or Dirty).

Users who are not the group owner/creator will receive an invite from the group owner/creator, after accepting the invite to the named group they will be able to view the other members of the group and will begin to receive notifications as other users in the same group as they update the status, state etc of the dishwasher. The entire group will be able to vote on (if they choose to) any purposed future cycles, allowing others to know if they have all their dishes etc ready to be put on and others to notify the group they are not ready.

Tools and Technologies

The android mobile application will operate similarly to applications like WhatsApp, so will likely need to be programmed in Java & JavaScript, HTML5 & CSS3 and Ruby for the mobile and backend development. To support the Automation framework of reminders and notifications RSpec, Capybara and PhantomJS are likely to be required. Databases and file storage services such as PostgreSQL will also be necessary, for hosting the application communications and data services like Amazons Web Services are the best option for allowing this. Security, Authentication end to end encryption will be vital, as users will likely be sharing potentially personal information, this would require the use of a third-party application programming interface (or API) like Nexmo which could be implemented via using phone numbers to authenticate new or current users. However, if we were taking the route of supporting email addresses and being able to link social media accounts to the application in some form tools such as Facebook SDK and Twitter Kit might be necessary (Shevchenko, Ruby Garage 2020), (Zakreskaya, Ruby Garage 2020).

In the area of hardware, compatible devices such as Windows PCs with sufficient hardware specs for development will be required and devices for testing.

Skills Required

A small team of skilled individuals will be needed for UI Design, Backend development and Android development with an understanding of the various programming languages and systems required for completion.

Significant time will be required for development and thus team members will need to have experience working on long term projects and all that comes with it when developing this application. Programmers and team members will also need to use and communicate through MS Teams, Discord and or Zoom to support development in the case that not all can be present.

Outcome

After reaching the point of successful completion, the app will be put onto platforms like Kickstarter to gain support and offered to groups of individuals for further feedback. Finally, after establishing a loyal base of users and support, the app will be released with a free version with either a form of subscription, monetization or similar to keep it running for the future.

The initial problem will be solved by allowing a more central way to go about planning and setting out times for, who, what, when and where the dishwasher will start and finish its cycle, who will be there or responsible for loading or unpacking. This will help eliminate or mitigate unplanned or mistimed cycles resolving disagreements and arguments at home.
